The sulfuric horizon is 15 cm or more thick and is composed of either mineral or organic soil material that has a pH value (1:1 by weight in water or in a minimum of water to permit measurement) of 3.5 or less or less than 4.0 if sulfide or other S-bearing minerals that produce sulfuric acid upon their oxidation are present. It has Jarosite concentrations (straw-yellow color, commonly hue of 2.5Y or yellower and chroma of > 6).
The horizon shows evidence that the low pH value is caused by sulfuric acid. The highly acidic materials are toxic to plants and negatively affect structures built upon them.
It is a diagnostic horizon for both mineral and organic soils.
