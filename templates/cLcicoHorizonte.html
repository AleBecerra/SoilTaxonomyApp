The calcic horizon is a subsoil horizon with a significant illuvial accumulation of calcium carbonate, whose evidence is either the presence of secondary forms, such as masses, threads coatings, pendants, or concretions, or nodules, or a Calcium carbonate equivalent that is higher than that of the underlying horizon.

Generalized Characteristics
   1) Thickness is > 15 cm.
   2) Calcium carbonate equivalent is > 15% (or > 5% if the horizon has < 18% clay and > 15% sand) and horizon either:
      a. Is 5% higher by weight (absolute) than an underlying horizon, or
      b. Has > 5%, by volume, visible secondary CaCO3 forms
   3) Horizon has no (or minimal) cementation.
