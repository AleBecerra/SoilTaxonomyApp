Contact between unconsolidated soil material and the underlying root-restrictive soft bedrock (paralithic materials).
Cracks that allow penetration by roots are spaced more than 10 cm apart.
The underlying bedrock is soft enough to make hand digging possible, although it may be difficult.

Paralithic materials
Cementation, bulk density, and the organization of these materials are such that roots cannot enter, except in cracks.
Commonly, these materials are partially weathered bedrock or weakly consolidated bedrock, such as sandstone, siltstone, or shale.
