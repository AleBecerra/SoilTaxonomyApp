Cation-exchange capacity (CEC)
It is the number of cmol(+) capable of being adsorbed by 1 kg of soil. That is to say, it is the sum of the positive charges that, in the form of cations of change, can adsorb a soil.
Because its value depends on the pH, it usually refers to the value determined at pH 7.

Effective CEC (ECEC)
When CEC measurement is done at the native soil pH.

Apparent CEC
This value is derived by dividing a CEC value of a whole soil sample by the percentage of clay in the sample. It is used to definde oxic and kandic horizons.
