(Gr. glossa, tongue). The glossic horizon is a degrading argillic, kandic, or natric horizon. In this horizon, eluviation of clay and iron oxides is occurring. This results in the formation of albic materials to the extent that they make up 15 to 85 percent of the volume of the horizon.
1. Horizon has thickness of > 5 cm.
2. Horizon has two distinct parts (eluvial and iluvial), each making up 15-85% of the horizon.
