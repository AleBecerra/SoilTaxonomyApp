A value that describes the ability of a saturated soil to support a load, such as grazing cattle or small machinery. It considers the percentage of water held under field conditions as well as percentages of silt, sand, clay, and organic matter in the soil.
For soil classification purposes, a critical n value of 0.7 is used.
A simple field test involves squeezing a fist-full of soil. If the soil flows between the fingers but with difficulty (i.e., slightly fluid), the n value is likely between 0.7 and 1.0. If the soil flows easily (i.e., moderately fluid or very fluid), it is greater than 1.0. If no soil flows between the fingers (non-fluid), it is less than .0.7.
